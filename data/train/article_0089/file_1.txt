The 2006 SPIE Symposium on Astronomical Telescopes and Instrumentation - Observing the Universe from Ground and Space took place from May 24 to 31 at the Orlando World Center Marriott Resort & Convention Center in Florida, USA. Over the past ten years, these biennial meetings have become a major venue for presenting and discussing various aspects of ground-based, airborne, and space telescopes and their tools, as well as relevant technological, software, operational advancements, and astronomical findings. Consequently, the events are large and attract many participants from different stages of starting, approving, implementing, and running astronomical projects and facilities. This year, about 1,700 participants registered and presented around 1,600 papers and posters in several parallel conferences crucial to the event.
