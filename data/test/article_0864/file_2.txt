To understand the changes in radial velocity, we look at variations in the shapes of photospheric spectral lines by examining the line bisector. We also assess chromospheric activity through the asymmetry of the Calcium II K line core, a key compressor indicator. Our study shows that for eight G and K giant stars, the changes in radial velocity are linked with asymmetry variation in the spectral line profiles and shifts in chromospheric Calcium II K line emission. An example of this relationship can be seen in the bisector velocity span versus radial velocity for the star HD 81797. Several of these stars are large in size, up to 20 milliarcseconds across, which the European Southern Observatory’s Very Large Telescope Interferometer could resolve. Hence, they qualify as good candidates for Very Large Telescope Interferometer observations, further allowing insights into patterns of surface irregularities via detailed assessments of fringe contrast across wavelengths.
