Detector Upgrade for FLAMES: GIRAFFE Gets Red Eyes  
In May 2008, a new CCD called Carreras was installed in the GIRAFFE spectrograph to replace the old detector, Bruce. Carreras is more sensitive to light at wavelengths longer than 700 nm. This report describes the main features and results from testing Carreras. FLAMES is the VLT'S multi-object fibre facility that offers a large corrected field of view (25 arc minutes in diameter) and includes several fibre modes. Most of these fibre modes connect to GIRAFFE, a medium-high resolution spectrograph that operates in the visible range (370 – 950 nm). Soon after operations began, we began searching for a new detector for GIRAFFE to improve the instrument's capability for red wavelengths while also maintaining good response for blue wavelengths and reducing noticeable fringing in the red spectrum. It took time to find a suitable device that met our strict requirements. Finally, e2v provided a superconducting deep depletion CCD with a custom anti-reflection coating, known as CCD44-82, custom coated by e2v and delivered to ESO in mid-2007, performing as expected.  
The new detector, Carreras, is a CCD44-82 with a resolution of 2 k × 4 k. It is identical in electrical and mechanical specifications to the existing detector (Bruce), so upgrading was quick and easy by using a plug-in replacement. Bruce is a standard silicon CCD44-82 with a nominal thickness of 16 µm and has a single layer anti-reflection coating designed for blue light. Carreras, the new detector, features a thicker nominal design of 40 µm implementing a two-layer precision coating (HfO/SiO2) optimized for broad wavelength responses between 370 – 950 nm. The upgrade the consisted of creating a new cryostat and installing Carreras with thorough characterization thereafter.
