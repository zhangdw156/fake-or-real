The Dwarf galaxy Abundances and Radial-velocities Team (DART) Large Program focuses on researching nearby dwarf spheroidal galaxies by analyzing numerous individual stars through ESO/WFI Imaging and VLT/FLAMES spectroscopy. These observations demonstrate that understanding the motion, composition, and arrangement of stars in these smaller galaxies is quite challenging. The primary finding highlights that dwarf galaxies endure unique and intricate evolutionary and metal-enrichment processes, especially from ancient times. This firmly indicates that these small galaxies aren't the foundational components of larger galaxies within the Local Group. The dwarf galaxies we have examined represent the lowest luminosity and mass now known.
