GRAAL for HAWK-I

Concept:



The Ground layer Adaptive Optics system Assisted by Lasers (GRAAL) is a module made to offer Ground Layer Adaptive Optics (GLAO) correction for a Near-Infrared (NIR) wide-field imager named HAWK-I. GRAAL consists of a unit with four wavefront sensors (WFSs) for Laser Guide Stars (LGS) and one tip-tilt sensor for a Natural Guide Star (NGS). It measures atmospheric turbulence from four slightly different angles within the instrument's field of view to send an average correction, making the view more uniform for scientific observations, to the Deformable Secondary Mirror (DSM).
