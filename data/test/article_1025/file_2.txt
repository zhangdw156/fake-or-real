The ESO Distant Cluster Survey (EDisCS) is a large project by ESO focused on examining galaxy clusters and their galaxies at redshifts between 0.4 and 1. Key issues explored include how star formation activities evolve in clusters, groups, and the surrounding field; whether this depends on the cluster mass or local galaxy density; the role of starburst and post-starburst galaxies in different settings; and if changes in galactic structures are connected to variations in star formation rates. EDisCS provides strong evidence that galaxy characteristics change with the environment. Star formation histories, galactic shapes, and masses show significant dependence on the area where galaxies are located at all explored redshifts.
