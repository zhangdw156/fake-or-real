Following a resolution in December 2005, there became a faster push to complete the study and have it reviewed by an international group by the year’s end. While the OWL Study has mostly been an internal ESO project, it has received important input from industry and a broader community in two key areas. First, in the last five years, a strong scientific rationale for a 50–100-meter Extremely Large Telescope (ELT) has been developed with Sof regarded to input from a larger group, thanks to European Commission programs FP5 and FP6. Second, in the past year, the initial identification and examination of an OWL instrument suite relevant to its scientific goals has been done through extensive community collaboration coordinated by ESO.
