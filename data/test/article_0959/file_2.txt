The Infra-Red Dual-beam Imaging and Spectroscopy (IRDIS) sub-system is the core scientific component of SPHERE. IRDIS is designed to operate within a spectral range of 950 to 2320 nm and has an image scale of 12.25 mas per pixel, aligning with Nyquist sampling at 950 nm. It requires a field of view (FOV) greater than 11 square arcseconds for both direct and dual imaging, which allows a little room for system optimization while utilizing two quadrants of a 2k × 2k detector. Its primary function is dual imaging, capturing images in two nearby spectral channels with minimized differences in sound quality (differential aberrations below 10 nm rms). There are ten pairs of filters available, each targeting different spectral features in simulated exoplanet spectra. In direct imaging mode, there are 12 filters of broad, medium, and narrow bandwidths. Alongside direct and dual imaging, there is also capability for long-slit spectroscopy with resolving powers of 50 and 500, and a dual polarimetric imaging mode. a pupil-imaging mode is available for system diagnostics.
