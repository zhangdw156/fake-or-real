The ONTHEFRINGE project sought to address the training shortfall by offering a coordinated and organized approach to European training in optical interferometry. One of the objectives of the training sessions was to teach a new wave of young astronomers to conduct scientific programs at the VLTI, including all steps from preparation to data processing and analysis. Additionally, another significant goal was to connect optical interferometry with other leading European astronomical techniques such as adaptive optics and radio/sub-mm interferometry. Unlike the NEON observing schools that occurred at observatories, the VLTI sessions were in more remote locations that still had adequate computing resources for practical training.
