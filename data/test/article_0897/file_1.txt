We successfully reapplied for observation time in Period 75 and were granted another opportunity, this time in Service Mode. We targeted various stars in NGC 6397, from the turnoff point to the red-giant branch. Each observation block allocated two UVES fibres to two stars situated in the middle of the subgiant branch, with one fibre observing the sky background. We observed five turnoff stars for a total of 12 hours, whereas six RGB stars needed only 1.5 hours to achieve a signal-to-noise ratio (S/N) of 100. The two subgiant stars, with a total observation time of 18 hours, had the best S/N. All 130 fibres for GIRAFFE were superconducting stars on the subgiant branch. Once the data was obtained, we began analysis, starting with surveying the effective temperatures of the turnoff stars. Early analysis indicated we were on the right track. It took several months to complete the full spectroscopic analysis of the 18 FLAMES-UVES targets. We could only derive meaningful results after correctly accounting for the sky background and providing fibre-to-fibre throughput corrections. By November 2005, the spectroscopic results were ready; we contacted Olivier Richard to compute diffusion models, considering radiative accelerations and turbulent mixing, to compare with the abundances we found. The analysis primarily focused on the extent of metal diffusion (namely iron) and the behavior of calcium since they limit the still-unknown strength of turbulent mixing.
