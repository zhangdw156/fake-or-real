The Visible and Infrared Survey Telescope for Astronomy (VISTA) is a 4-meter telescope designed for observing near-infrared light and will feature 16 infrared detectors that cover a 1.5 square degree area. Figure 4 displays the VIRCAM field of view, with the moon added for scale. The telescope uses various filters: Z, Y, J, H, Ks, and a narrowband filter operating at 1.18 microns. Each shot will capture 67 Megapixels, and an average observing night will result in collecting 300 GB of data. Processing this large volume of data will be crucial and will require significant resources.
